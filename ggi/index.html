In April 2021, the United States and Canada announced a new effort—the Greening Government Initiative (GGI)— to engage governments around the world in greening government operations. This first-of-its-kind international community of practice will enable countries to share lessons learned, promote innovation, and help meet Paris Agreement commitments.

The Greening Government Initiative will serve as a platform for country representatives to share information and best practices, showcase innovation and success, and develop collaborative relationships with one another to accelerate national efforts to green government operations including climate resilience.

Common areas of interest could include activities such as increasing use of renewable energy, transitioning national fleets to zero emission vehicles, electrifying government buildings, establishing sustainable procurement strategies and improving resilience of government infrastructure.

The GGI will host its inaugural meeting on September 22, 2021. The meeting is open to all interested countries and will occur virtually. A readout of the meeting as well as other relevant meeting materials will be posted online afterwards.

Please contact GGI@ceq.eop.gov for more information about GGI.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the Greening Government Initiative (GGI)?

GGI is a global effort to stand up a community of practice for leaders working to develop and deploy national sustainability efforts. Building a network of government sustainability leaders will increase opportunities for cooperation and information sharing, which will accelerate sustainability efforts across all participating countries.

What are the desired outcomes of GGI?

Through this effort, we seek to:

  • Build a community of practice
  • Accelerate greening government efforts worldwide
  • Leverage international partnerships and utilize economies of scale
  • Strengthen foundation for industry collaboration
  • Amplify experimentation and innovation
  • Create momentum and inspire continuous GG improvement
What topics will GGI cover?

The scope of GGI is limited to national government operations. This includes government buildings, fleets, workplaces, procurement, etc. We explicitly distinguish this effort as related to, but separate from, efforts such as nationally determined contributions (NDCs), which focus on sectors outside government operations.

What is the right level for involvement?

The target of this effort is leaders and teams directly responsible for the development and execution of national government sustainability / greening operations.

What other countries are involved?

Participation is expected to be broad as there is no cost or commitment required beyond participation at events. Over 100 countries have responded to the initial call for a point of contact (POC).

How do countries get involved?

There will be periodic sessions for countries to convene, share ongoing efforts, present initiatives, discuss new ways to work together, and help each other recognize and address challenges involved in the process of greening national government operations. Ahead of these sessions, there will be opportunities to suggest ideas, initiatives, challenges or other topics of interest to the community. Email GGI@ceq.eop.gov if you are interested in future meetings.

What level of commitment is required by participants?

There is no particular set of commitments. The GGI is a community—a place where people navigating similar issues can engage in conversation to support best practices and further national objectives. The initiative is designed to be a convening mechanism for those responsible for greening government operations.

Are individual country representatives required (or able to) provide statements or talking points?

There is nothing required of representatives other than attending convenings and engaging with the community. However, there will be plenty of opportunities to lead, comment, respond to, and add to discussions within the community. We hope all nations will participate in bringing ideas to the group.

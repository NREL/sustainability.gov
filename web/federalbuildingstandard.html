On December 7, 2022, the Biden-Harris Administration announced the first-ever energy and climate performance standard for the country's 300,000 Federal buildings. As the single largest energy consumer and building manager in the nation, the Federal Building Performance Standard (BPS) will help the U.S. Government lower its energy bills, increase resilience, cut emissions, create new markets, grow good-paying jobs, and demonstrate leadership to reduce climate change.

The Federal BPS requires each agency to eliminate on-site fossil fuel use in 30 percent of their existing buildings through energy efficient, cost-effective electrification of equipment and appliances. The Federal BPS works in conjunction with statutory Federal building fossil fuel reduction requirements and other building-related goals—such as reducing energy and water use intensity, implementing deep energy retrofits, procuring carbon-pollution free electricity, and prioritizing project implementation to address environmental justice and climate adaptation planning—which will not only reduce costs and maximize efficiencies, but will ensure that the social and economic benefits of these interventions extends beyond the walls of federal buildings.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a Building Performance Standard?

A Building Performance Standard (BPS) is an outcome-based policy and law aimed at reducing the carbon impact of the built environment by requiring existing buildings to meet energy- or GHG emissions-based performance targets.

Why focus on reducing emissions from buildings?

In the United States, buildings account for nearly 40 percent of both total energy consumption and greenhouse gas emissions. Therefore, reducing building energy use and emissions is critical to tackling climate change and strengthening the U.S. energy independence and national security.

That's why President Biden charged his Administration through his December 2021 Federal Sustainability Plan and Executive Order 14057 to lead by example to achieve a net-zero emissions buildings portfolio by 2045, including a 50 percent emissions ruction by 2032. Implementing the Federal BPS is one tool the Administration will use to reach building emissions reduction goal.

How does the Federal BPS integrate with other net zero emissions buildings related goals for Federal buildings?

Over several decades, the Federal Government has established policy and guidance to improve the energy efficiency and sustainability of Federal buildings. These include energy efficiency standards for new construction, implementation of facility-level metering infrastructure, identification and implementation of efficiency measures, use of performance contracting to drive overall reductions in portfolio energy use and emissions, and more. The Federal BPS represents another tool to use to meet the President's Federal sustainability goals.

What is the National Building Performance Standards Coalition?

Launched by President Biden, the National Building Performance Standards Coalition comprises a nationwide group of state and local governments that have committed to inclusively design and implement building performance policies and programs in their jurisdictions. Coalition members are supported by federal agencies, labor, and non-governmental organizations which provide resources for location-based workforce engagement, technical analysis, equity strategies, localized policy design, and stakeholder engagement.

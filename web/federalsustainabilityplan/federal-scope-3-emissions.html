Federal Scope 3 GHG Emissions Inventory

The Federal government is leading by example by publishing its Scope 3 greenhouse gas (GHG) emissions inventory, committing to a target of 30% reduction by 2030, and highlighting supplier progress on key greenhouse gas emissions and climate risk management activities.

Fiscal Year 2023 Emissions by Source

  Source FY 2023 Emissions1 Percent of Total Scope 3
1 Supply Chain 120,900,831 89.2%
2 Fuel and Energy Related 7,891,822 5.8%
3 Employee Commuting 2,442,088 1.8%
4 Upstream Leased Assets 2,205,941 1.6%
5 Business Travel 1,779,753 1.3%
6 Waste Disposal 357,536 0.3%
1Metric tons of carbon dioxide equivalent (MTCO2e)

Visit the Federal Scope 3 emissions data dashboard for more details.

 

Breaking Down Scope 3 Emissions by Source

Supply Chain
120,900,831 MTCO2e
Categories 1, 2, 4

Emissions resulting from federal expenditures (contract dollars obligated) for all goods and services not included in other Scope 3 categories.

Fuel and Energy Related
7,891,822 MTCO2e
Category 3

Emissions from the production, transmission, and delivery of fuels and energy used for federal buildings and vehicles.

Employee Commuting
2,442,088 MTCO2e
Category 7

Emissions from employees’ daily commuting between home and work using personal vehicles and public transportation.

Upstream Leased Assets
2,205,941 MTCO2e
Category 8

Emissions from fuel and electricity used to power leased assets, including leased offices, warehouses, and other buildings.

Business Travel
1,779,753 MTCO2e
Category 6

Emissions from business-related travel, in commercial or personal vehicles, by ground (e.g., car, bus, train) and air.

Waste Disposal
357,536 MTCO2e
Category 5

Emissions produced in the hauling and breakdown of solid waste and processing of wastewater from Federal facilities.

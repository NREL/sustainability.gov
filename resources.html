Resources and Guidance for Federal Agencies

 Home / Resources

Listed below are resources and guidance Federal agencies can use to address environmental and sustainability priorities.

Council on Environmental Quality Guidance

  • Federal Renewable Energy Certificate Guide (June 2016)
  • Federal Greenhouse Gas Accounting and Reporting Guidance (January 2016)
  • Guiding Principles for Sustainable Federal Buildings and Associated Instructions (February 2016)
  • Determining Compliance with the Guiding Principles for Sustainable Federal Buildings (February 2016)
  • Guidance for Federal Agency Implementation of Workplace Charging Pursuant to the Fixing America's Surface Transportation Act: Level 1 Charging Receptacles (June 2016)
  • Guidance for Federal Agency Implementation of Workplace Charging Pursuant to the Fixing America's Surface Transportation Act: Electric Vehicle Supply Equipment (October 2016)
  • Interim Guidance for Calculating Federal Compliance with Waste Diversion Goals (December 2016)

Websites

Facility, Energy, and Water Management

  • The U.S. Department of Energy's Federal Energy Management Program (FEMP) website provides Federal agencies with information, guidance, training on laws and requirements, project financing, renewable energy, water efficiency, data centers, and energy-efficient products and technologies.
  • The General Services Administration (GSA) maintains the comprehensive and interactive Sustainable Facilities Tool, a gateway for trainings, case studies, and more on running buildings efficiently.

Fleet Management

  • FEMP's Federal Fleet Management website provides a wide range of resources for Federal agencies on fleet management, petroleum reduction, alternative fuel use, alternative fuel vehicle acquisition, and reporting requirements.
  • GSA's Office of Fleet Management website offers Federal purchasing and leasing information for vehicles. Visit this website for training, contact experts, and learn about sustainable fleets.
  • GSA also provides the Federal Fleet Management System, which Federal agencies can deploy to maintain and report on their fleets.
  • The U.S. Department of Transportation offers information about alternative fuel corridors.

Products and Technologies

  • GSA's Green Procurement Compilation provides access to comprehensive information on sustainable procurement requirements, tools, and best practices in one place. Visit to see a database of compliant products, learn about requirements, download the catalog, and find recommended language to include in contracts.
  • The U.S. Environmental Protection Agency's Greener Products website provides information about the standards, specifications, and labels that identify products to meet requirements and achieve sustainable acquisition goals.
  • The U.S. Department of Agriculture manages the BioPreferred products program. Visit this website to learn about biobased purchasing requirements and designated products.
  • Find information on product energy-efficiency requirements through the Energy Star website and on the FEMP website.
